# 100 Viral Hooks — Swipe File

The top-performing spoken hooks from creators across every niche. Each hook includes the framework (so you can adapt it to YOUR business) and the structure type.

Pick a hook. Swap in your topic. Use it as-is or feed it into Claude for content.

---

## SECRET REVEAL HOOKS
*These hooks tease hidden knowledge or insider info. The viewer stays because they want to know the secret.*

**1.** "Do you want to [X]? Do you want to [Y]? Do you want to [Z]? Chances are you want to [bigger goal], but might not have [obstacle]. Introducing [solution]."
> *Framework: Stack desires → reveal obstacle → introduce solution*

**2.** "Nobody [failed action] harder than [example]."
> *Framework: Superlative failure → creates curiosity about what happened*

**3.** "Check this out, this [person] is building [project]. But to do it, they had to [unexpected requirement]. It's insane."
> *Framework: Cool project → unexpected twist → hype*

**4.** "[Person's] [achievement] might be the most [superlative] in [field] history."
> *Framework: Bold claim about a specific person's record*

**5.** "Check this out. These are called [innovation]. You can literally [capability]. And I think this is the future of how people are gonna [activity]."
> *Framework: Name the innovation → show capability → future prediction*

**6.** "This prompt has been going viral — [intriguing question]."
> *Framework: Social proof ("going viral") → curiosity gap*

**7.** "Check this out. This company lets you [impossible-sounding service]. It's like [familiar service] for [unexpected thing]."
> *Framework: Impossible claim → relatable analogy*

**8.** "The [person] [discovers your success] and can't stand [your achievement], so [their reaction]. You tell them that [your secret method]."
> *Framework: Jealousy scenario → reveal the secret*

**9.** "The tech in [venue] is insane. [Impressive fact]. But get this, [expected highlight] is actually the least impressive part because the most impressive part is [unexpected element]."
> *Framework: Set expectation → flip it → blow their mind*

**10.** "This company [method] an entire [project at scale]. But get this, because they're [method], people can [unexpected benefit]."
> *Framework: Scale reveal → unexpected upside*

**11.** "There's a model called [concept], and here's the wild part."
> *Framework: Name-drop a framework → tease the twist*

**12.** "[Company] just dropped the [product] with something we didn't expect, but before you [action], let's take a look."
> *Framework: Product launch → curiosity gap → slow reveal*

**13.** "Something crazy is happening with [product]. You ever seen one of these? [Product], racking up [metric]. This [description] has become the [luxury brand] of [category]."
> *Framework: Trend alert → social proof → bold comparison*

**14.** "Most people have no idea you can legally [surprising action] and it's much easier to do than you think."
> *Framework: "Most people don't know" → legal loophole → easy*

**15.** "[Company] just announced the [product] which has some pretty insane [features] we haven't seen on any other [category]."
> *Framework: Announcement → exclusive features → FOMO*

**16.** "These [people] made [amount] just by [controversial method], and they never [consequence], and you'll see why."
> *Framework: Controversial success → no punishment → curiosity*

**17.** "The fact that [person] was even able to [achievement] is nothing short of incredible."
> *Framework: Underdog setup → emotional hook*

**18.** "If you create any kind of [work] for [clients] then you're going to want to hear about this new feature from [company]."
> *Framework: Target audience → "you need to hear this"*

**19.** "Yeah, this is utterly insane. [Subject] is 100% [shocking fact]."
> *Framework: Shock statement → reveal*

**20.** "And boom. With zero technical skills you can [create output] with [tool]."
> *Framework: Zero barrier → impressive result → tool reveal*

**21.** "Here are [number] websites that are completely free that every [audience] needs to know."
> *Framework: Free resources → targeted audience → FOMO*

**22.** "Why do most [things] look like [bad result]? Because [simple reason]."
> *Framework: Common problem → simple root cause*

**61.** "Should you [activity] with or without [variable]?"

## How to Use These Hooks

1. **Pick a hook** that fits your content topic
2. **Swap in your specifics** — your niche, your product, your audience
3. **Say it out loud** — if it sounds natural when you speak it, it'll work on camera
4. **First 3 seconds matter most** — the hook IS the first 3 seconds
5. **Feed it to Claude** — paste a hook + your topic and let Claude build the full script

These hooks work for short-form (TikTok, Reels, Shorts), long-form intros (YouTube), ad creative, email subject lines, and social captions. The framework is the weapon — the topic is the ammo.
