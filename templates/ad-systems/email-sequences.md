## Sequence 1: Welcome Series (5 Emails)

New subscriber just joined your list. These 5 emails turn a stranger into a fan who's ready to buy.

**Timing:** Email 1 = immediate. Email 2 = Day 2. Email 3 = Day 4. Email 4 = Day 6. Email 5 = Day 8.

---

### Email 1: The Welcome (Immediate)

**Subject line options:**
- "You're in. Here's what to expect."
- "Welcome — let me tell you what you just signed up for"
- "[FIRST NAME], you made a good call"

**Body outline:**
1. Welcome them — keep it warm, keep it real (2-3 sentences)
2. Tell them who you are in ONE sentence — not your life story
3. Set expectations — what emails they'll get and how often
4. Give them a quick win RIGHT NOW — [LINK TO YOUR BEST FREE RESOURCE / TIP / VIDEO]
5. Sign off with personality — not "Best regards"

**CTA:** "Hit reply and tell me [SIMPLE QUESTION — e.g., 'what's your biggest challenge right now?']"

---

### Email 2: Your Story (Day 2)

**Subject line options:**
- "Why I do this (the real reason)"
- "I almost didn't start this business"
- "The story nobody asks about"

**Body outline:**
1. Hook — start with the moment that changed everything (1-2 sentences)
2. The struggle — what you were dealing with before [YOUR BUSINESS] (3-4 sentences)
3. The turning point — what happened that made you start (2-3 sentences)
4. Where you are now — brief, not braggy (1-2 sentences)
5. Connection — "If you've ever felt [RELATABLE FEELING], that's exactly why I built this"

**CTA:** "Stick around — tomorrow I'm sharing [TEASER FOR EMAIL 3]"

---

### Email 3: Best Content / Resource (Day 4)

**Subject line options:**
- "My most saved [post/video/guide] ever"
- "If you only read one thing from me, make it this"
- "This [resource] changed the game for my clients"

**Body outline:**
1. Context — "I've created a lot of content, but this one keeps coming up" (1-2 sentences)
2. What it is and why it matters to THEM (2-3 sentences)
3. Key takeaway they'll get from it (1-2 bullet points)
4. Link to the resource

**CTA:** Direct link to [YOUR TOP-PERFORMING BLOG POST / VIDEO / GUIDE / SOCIAL POST]

---

### Email 4: Common Mistake (Day 6)

**Subject line options:**
- "The #1 mistake I see [YOUR AUDIENCE] make"
- "This is probably costing you [MONEY/TIME/RESULTS]"
- "If you're doing this, stop"

**Body outline:**
1. Name the mistake directly — no beating around the bush (1-2 sentences)
2. Why people make it — it's not their fault, it's [COMMON REASON] (2-3 sentences)
3. What to do instead — give the actual fix, not a tease (3-4 sentences)
4. Quick proof — "When my client [NAME/ANONYMOUS] stopped doing this, [RESULT]" (1-2 sentences)

**CTA:** "Want help fixing this? [SOFT PITCH — reply to this email / check out my free resource / etc.]"

---

### Email 5: The Soft Pitch (Day 8)

**Subject line options:**
- "Can I help you with something?"
- "I built [YOUR OFFER] for people exactly like you"
- "This is for you if [CONDITION]"

**Body outline:**
1. Recap the journey — "Over the last week I've shared [brief summary]" (1-2 sentences)
2. The gap — "But knowing and doing are different things" (1-2 sentences)
3. Introduce your offer naturally — "That's why I created [OFFER NAME]" (2-3 sentences)
4. What they get — 3-5 bullet points of outcomes (not features)
5. Social proof — one testimonial or result (2 sentences)
6. Clear CTA

**CTA:** "[LINK TO OFFER / BOOKING PAGE / SALES PAGE]"

## Sequence 2: Launch Sequence (7 Emails)

You're launching something — a product, service, course, program. These 7 emails build hype and drive sales.

**Timing:** Emails 1-2 = pre-launch week. Emails 3-5 = launch week. Emails 6-7 = last chance.

---

### Email 1: The Seed (5-7 days before launch)

**Subject:** "Something's coming"

**Body outline:**
1. Tease what you've been working on — don't reveal it yet (2-3 sentences)
2. Why you built it — the problem you kept seeing (2-3 sentences)
3. "I'll have all the details for you on [LAUNCH DATE]"

---

### Email 2: The Problem (3-4 days before launch)

**Subject:** "Why [COMMON APPROACH] isn't working for you"

**Body outline:**
1. Call out the problem your offer solves — be specific (2-3 sentences)
2. Why existing solutions fall short (2-3 sentences)
3. "I've been building something that fixes this. Details dropping [DATE]."

---

### Email 3: Launch Day

**Subject:** "It's live. [OFFER NAME] is here."

**Body outline:**
1. Announce it — clear, excited, direct (1-2 sentences)
2. What it is in plain English (2-3 sentences)
3. Who it's for — "This is for you if..." (3-4 bullet points)
4. What's included (4-6 bullet points — outcomes, not features)
5. Price + any launch bonus
6. CTA — big and clear

---

### Email 4: The Deep Dive (Launch day +1)

**Subject:** "Here's exactly what you get with [OFFER NAME]"

**Body outline:**
1. Full breakdown of everything included
2. Walk through each component — what it is, why it matters
3. Address the "is this worth it?" question head-on
4. Testimonial or case study if you have one
5. CTA

---

### Email 5: Objection Killer (Launch day +2)

**Subject:** "I know what you're thinking"

**Body outline:**
1. Name the top 3 objections your audience has — price, time, "will this work for me?"
2. Answer each one honestly — don't be slimy about it
3. "If none of those are your reason, hit reply and tell me what's holding you back"
4. CTA

---

### Email 6: Social Proof (Launch day +4)

**Subject:** "[NAME] just [RESULT] — here's what they said"

**Body outline:**
1. Share 2-3 testimonials, results, or screenshots
2. Brief context for each one
3. "These are real people who were exactly where you are right now"
4. CTA — "Join them"

---

### Email 7: Last Chance (Final day)

**Subject:** "Last call — [OFFER] closes tonight"

**Body outline:**
1. Deadline is real — state it clearly (1 sentence)
2. Quick recap of what they get (bullet points)
3. "After tonight, [WHAT HAPPENS — price goes up / doors close / bonus disappears]"
4. One final testimonial or result
5. Urgent CTA

## Sequence 3: Abandoned Cart (3 Emails)

They added to cart but didn't buy. These 3 emails bring them back.

**Timing:** Email 1 = 1 hour after abandonment. Email 2 = 24 hours. Email 3 = 48 hours.

---

### Email 1: The Gentle Nudge (1 hour)

**Subject line options:**
- "You left something behind"
- "Still thinking about it?"
- "Your cart's getting lonely"

**Body outline:**
1. Keep it simple — "Looks like you didn't finish checking out" (1 sentence)
2. Show them what's in their cart — [PRODUCT NAME + IMAGE if your platform supports it]
3. "If you had a question or something went wrong, just reply to this email"
4. CTA — "Complete your order"

---

### Email 2: Handle the Hesitation (24 hours)

**Subject line options:**
- "Quick question about your order"
- "Was it the price? The shipping? Something else?"
- "Before you decide — read this"

**Body outline:**
1. Acknowledge they might be on the fence — that's normal (1-2 sentences)
2. Address the top reason people hesitate — [SHIPPING / PRICE / "WILL IT WORK?"] (2-3 sentences)
3. Add social proof — one review or testimonial (2 sentences)
4. Sweeten the deal if you can — [FREE SHIPPING / DISCOUNT CODE / BONUS] (optional)
5. CTA — "Complete your order"

---

### Email 3: Final Push (48 hours)

**Subject line options:**
- "Last chance to grab [PRODUCT NAME]"
- "Your cart expires soon"
- "I'll be honest — this is my last email about this"

**Body outline:**
1. Be direct — "This is my last reminder" (1 sentence)
2. Quick value recap — why this product is worth it (2-3 bullet points)
3. If you offered a discount, remind them it expires (1 sentence)
4. "If it's not for you, no hard feelings. But if you've been meaning to grab it — now's the time."
5. CTA — "Complete your order"
